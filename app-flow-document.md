# App Flow Document

## Main User Journey

The website should guide a local business owner from quick understanding to contact.

1. Visitor lands on the home page.
2. Visitor immediately sees the main offer and target audience.
3. Visitor chooses either to contact you or explore packages.
4. Visitor scans services and use cases.
5. Visitor compares packages.
6. Visitor reads process and trust signals.
7. Visitor sends a lead through WhatsApp, phone, or a contact form.

## Navigation Flow

Primary navigation:

- Services
- Packages
- Process
- Work
- FAQ
- Contact

Primary button:

- Get Free Plan

Secondary actions:

- View Packages
- WhatsApp
- Book a Call

## Home Page Flow

### 1. Header

Purpose:

Give users quick access to sections and make the contact action always available.

Elements:

- Logo or text brand
- Navigation links
- WhatsApp/contact button

### 2. Hero

Purpose:

Explain what you sell and who it is for.

Elements:

- Clear headline
- Short supporting paragraph
- Primary CTA
- Secondary CTA
- Trust points such as "Mobile-ready", "Lead-focused", "Built for local property businesses"
- Visual showing a building/property website mockup or real estate image with lead-stat cards

### 3. Trust Strip

Purpose:

Make the offer feel credible.

Possible content:

- Built for builders
- Property managers
- Rental businesses
- Local SEO
- WhatsApp leads

### 4. Services

Purpose:

Show what the client can buy.

Service cards:

- Builder websites
- Property management websites
- Property/project showcase
- Enquiry forms
- Maintenance request forms
- WhatsApp lead capture

### 5. Use Cases

Purpose:

Help different customer types see themselves in the offer.

Use-case tabs or cards:

- For builders: show projects, collect buyer enquiries, present completed work
- For property managers: collect tenant requests, show available rentals, reduce phone calls
- For landlords: present units, collect applications, centralize communication

### 6. Packages

Purpose:

Move visitors toward a buying decision.

Package cards:

- Starter
- Growth
- Pro

Each package should include:

- Who it is for
- Key features
- Starting price or quote CTA
- Button

### 7. Process

Purpose:

Make it feel easy to start.

Steps:

1. Free planning call
2. Content and structure
3. Design preview
4. Build and launch
5. Support and improvements

### 8. Testimonials / Proof

Purpose:

Build confidence.

If real testimonials are not available yet, use "sample outcomes" instead of fake reviews.

Example:

- "Launch a professional website in days, not months."
- "Turn WhatsApp into a clear lead channel."
- "Give tenants one place to send requests."

### 9. FAQ

Purpose:

Handle objections.

Questions:

- How long does a website take?
- Do I need to provide photos?
- Can you add WhatsApp?
- Can you make a site for a single building project?
- Can you update my old website?
- Do you provide hosting?

### 10. Final CTA

Purpose:

Give one clear next step.

CTA:

"Get a Free Website Plan"

Supporting text:

"Tell me your business type and I will suggest the best website structure for your local market."

## Lead Flow

Preferred lead routes:

1. WhatsApp button opens a pre-filled message.
2. Contact form collects name, business type, phone, city, and project need.
3. Phone/email links are available in footer.

## Contact Form Fields

- Name
- Business name
- Business type
- Phone or WhatsApp number
- City/local area
- What do you need?

Business type options:

- Builder
- Property manager
- Landlord
- Real estate agent
- Other

## Mobile Flow

Mobile users should be able to:

- Understand the offer without horizontal scrolling
- Tap WhatsApp quickly
- View packages in one column
- Submit a short form
- Navigate through a compact menu

## Empty / Missing Content Flow

If real portfolio or testimonials are not available, show:

- Sample website concepts
- Package examples
- Process explanation
- Clear invitation for a free plan

Avoid fake customer names or fake testimonials.
